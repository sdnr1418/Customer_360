**What to Do:**

**What NOT to do:**
- [ ] Don't rush
- [ ] Don't apologize ("I'm sorry if this is long...")
- [ ] Don't look nervous (even if you are!)

### During Content (5 slides in)

- [ ] Speak clearly and slowly
- [ ] Make eye contact (scan the room)
- [ ] Show passion about findings (41x is impressive!)
- [ ] If you lose your place: pause, look at notes, continue
- [ ] Use CHEAT_SHEET.md if needed (glance is fine)

### When They Interrupt/Ask Questions

- [ ] Listen fully to the entire question
- [ ] Take 1-2 seconds to think
- [ ] Answer directly and briefly (20-30 seconds)
- [ ] If complex: check PROFESSOR_PRESENTATION.md or say "That's a great question. Let me check my notes."
- [ ] Reference CHEAT_SHEET.md for pre-written responses

### If Something Goes Wrong

**Problem: PowerPoint crashes**
```
Solution: Open VISUAL_SUMMARY.md on screen
Get back on track: "Let me rebuild that slide real quick.
In the meantime, here's the summary."
Continue presenting from memory + notes
```

**Problem: You lose your train of thought**
```
Solution: "Let me make sure I'm clear on my point."
Pause for 3 seconds
Look at PRESENTATION_OUTLINE.md
Continue with next section
```

**Problem: They ask something outside your scope**
```
Solution: "That's a great question and definitely relevant.
That's actually Phase 3 work, but I've noted it down."
Then move on.
```

**Problem: They challenge your methodology**
```
Solution: Stay calm. Say "That's a fair point. What we DID do was...
[explain what you did]. You're right that A/B testing is the real proof."
Check PROFESSOR_PRESENTATION.md-Limitations for details if needed.
```
